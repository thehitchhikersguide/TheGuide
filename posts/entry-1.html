The Surprisingly Inconvenient Nature of Learning Things

According to most books, learning is supposed to be a noble and inspiring activity. According to the universe, however, it is more like trying to assemble flat-pack furniture using instructions written by a raccoon who has never seen a hex key.

Today's lesson came from a book insisting that “small habits can change your life,” which sounds very convincing but fails to mention that the *small habits* in question include things like remembering where you put the book in the first place.

Still, the process was enlightening. Mostly because I read it near a window, and sunlight was involved. But also because each chapter quietly reminded me that humans, for all their ambition, routinely forget why they walked into a room.

Note: if the universe ever offers you a shortcut to self-improvement, it is either a trap, a pyramid scheme, or both.
